# Academic Writing Checklist

This checklist is designed to help writers review academic work before submission.
It focuses on clarity, structure, reasoning, and alignment with assessment expectations.

Use it as a final review tool rather than a writing guide.

## Question understanding
- The essay directly addresses the question
- Key terms in the question are clearly understood
- The response stays focused on the task throughout

## Central argument
- A clear thesis or position is stated
- The argument remains consistent
- The position is specific and defensible

## Structure and organisation
- The introduction sets clear direction
- Body paragraphs follow a logical order
- Each paragraph focuses on one main idea
- Transitions connect ideas smoothly
- The conclusion reinforces the argument

## Paragraph quality
- Each paragraph has a clear topic sentence
- Evidence is introduced and explained
- Analysis connects evidence to the argument
- Paragraphs are balanced in length

## Use of evidence
- Evidence supports claims directly
- Sources are relevant and appropriate
- Evidence is explained rather than listed
- Overuse of quotations is avoided

## Reasoning and analysis
- Ideas are analysed, not just described
- Assumptions are questioned where appropriate
- Counterarguments are acknowledged and addressed
- Evaluation shows depth of thinking

## Clarity and focus
- Language is clear and precise
- Sentences are easy to follow
- Unnecessary information is removed
- Repetition is avoided unless it develops the argument

## Logical flow
- Ideas progress naturally
- Paragraph order supports the argument
- There are no abrupt shifts in direction

## Conclusion quality
- The conclusion summarises key points
- The central argument is restated clearly
- No new ideas are introduced

## Final review
- The essay meets word count requirements
- Formatting follows academic guidelines
- Citations are consistent and accurate
- Grammar and spelling are checked

## Why this checklist matters
Using a structured checklist improves revision quality.
It helps writers focus on clarity, reasoning, and academic standards rather than surface-level edits.

Strong academic writing is built through careful review and refinement.
